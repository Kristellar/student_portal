BIGGEST RISKS TO MOBILE APPS SECURITY 2024

  • Home
  • /
  • Blog
Feb 15, 2024 1 Comment Kristellar Team

The biggest mobile security risk we have today is rooted devices: iPhones, Android phones. Once you do that, there is no guarantee of a secure ecosystem, specifically around mobile devices. The biggest risk is the lack of people updating their devices when new operating systems come out. When they do that, they not only compromise company data, but they compromise their own data.

Loss of data

It is the biggest security risk to mobile -- in fact, any endpoint. If you have employees bringing in their devices to work and joining the network, then what kind of paths do those devices have to the data center? To me, the devices aren't that special -- yes, they cost money, and if they get lost or stolen, that's a loss but the biggest risk here is that the data is compromised.
Ensuring top notch mobile apps security for your business application is crucial to preserve and improve your business reputation.It is important to ensure that user data is secured properly in available app. In the case of failure to provide good security precious data can be stolen or manipulated,the avalabel app can be injected with a virus, it can be cloned, and a doppelganger brand can emerge for your company. Ultimately you may end up wasting all your resources spent in development and end up ruining your business reputation.

Weak server controls and no binary protection make apps vulnerable to hacking. Use binary hardening and secure coding to protect against threats.

Top Risks to Mobile Apps Security and Ways to Secure Your Apps:

Insecure Data Storage: Relying on client-side storage exposes mobile apps to data breaches if the device is compromised. To enhance security, add an extra layer of encryption beyond the OS's default.

Poor Authorization and Authentication: Weak authentication, often due to short passwords or offline modes, allows unauthorized access. To prevent breaches, limit logins to online modes or encrypt offline data.

Unintended Data Leakage: Storing critical app data in easily accessible locations leads to privacy breaches. Prevent leakage by monitoring common points like caching, logging, and browser storage.

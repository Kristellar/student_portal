CYBER THREATS TO INDIA POWER GRID, OIL & GAS SECTORS 2024

  • Home
  • /
  • Blog
April 10, 2024 1 Comment Kristellar Team

The oil and gas industry is a prime target for adversaries seeking to exploit Industrial Control Systems (ICS) environments. As attacks on ICS systems increase, those specifically targeting oil and gas companies are becoming more active and evolving their tactics. SHADOWTRACE has identified a new activity group focused on this sector, with five distinct groups concentrating specifically on oil and gas. This report offers a snapshot of the threat landscape as of July 2024 and notes that the situation is likely to evolve as adversaries adapt their strategies.

Key findings indicate that the ICS security risk to the global oil and gas sector is both high and escalating. Numerous intrusions into ICS networks for reconnaissance and research, as well as the deployment of destructive malware at oil and gas facilities, contribute to this heightened risk. Given the sector's political and economic importance and its inherently volatile processes, it remains highly susceptible to potentially destructive cyberattacks.

Effective cybersecurity in energy sectors demands advanced strategies like binary hardening and proactive threat intelligence.

In the first half of 2024, ShadowTrace's assessment revealed an increasing likelihood that state-associated actors will target the oil and gas industry and related sectors to further their political, economic, and national security objectives. Cybersecurity visibility in oil and gas operational environments is critically inadequate, with recent attack vectors allowing intrusions to persist longer and making post-incident cyber root cause analysis challenging.

The complete "energy infrastructure" — including oil, gas, electric, and other sectors — faces global threats. Companies and utilities are encountering adversaries that operate across these interconnected sectors. As activity groups target original equipment manufacturers, third-party vendors, and telecommunications providers, it's clear that these sectors can no longer be protected in isolation.

To mitigate these threats, employing binary hardening techniques is crucial. Binary hardening involves analyzing and modifying binary files to protect against common exploits, thereby addressing vulnerabilities in legacy code without needing source code access. Additionally, secure coding practices, such as jailbreak detection controls, checksum controls, certificate pinning, and debugger detection, should be implemented to enhance application security.

In conclusion, Threat Intelligence Platforms like SHADOWTRACE represent an emerging technology that can significantly benefit organizations by enabling them to act on cyber intelligence. Rather than relying solely on external sources, organizations can leverage SHADOWTRACE to develop their own actionable intelligence, providing a more reliable, sustainable, and cost-effective approach for long-term cybersecurity defenses.
